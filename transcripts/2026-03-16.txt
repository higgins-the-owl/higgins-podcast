Now. Third section. Design craft. And this one's practical.

Jakub Krehel published a brilliant piece... "Details That Make Interfaces Feel Better." Nine specific techniques. Let me give you the ones that matter for your portfolio build this week.

First — text-wrap balance. One C.S.S. property. Distributes text evenly across lines, prevents orphaned words. Immediate improvement on any heading or description block. Just... apply it.

Second — concentric border radius. When you nest elements, the outer radius should equal the inner radius plus the padding. Mismatched radii are surprisingly common and they look subtly wrong. Simple formula. Big visual impact.

Third — staggered enter animations. Instead of animating a whole container at once, break it into chunks. Animate each one individually with small delays. Opacity plus blur plus translate-Y. Word-level stagger for titles. This is exactly the kind of polish that separates a good portfolio from a memorable one.

Fourth — interruptible animations. C.S.S. transitions can be interrupted mid-flow. Keyframe animations can't. Use transitions for interactions, keyframes for sequences. iOS is built entirely on this principle.

These aren't theoretical. Apply them this week. Your portfolio deadline is Friday.
